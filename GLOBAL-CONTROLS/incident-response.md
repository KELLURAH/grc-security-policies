# Incident Response Controls

**Effective Date:** December 6, 2026  
**Control Owner:** Security or Compliance Lead  
**Target Audience:** All employees, contractors, and long-term vendors with system access  
**Classification:** Internal Use

---

## 1. Purpose

Establish a consistent, timely, and auditable process for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

---

## 2. Scope

These controls apply to:

- All systems, networks, applications, and data owned or managed by the company
- All personnel, contractors, and vendors with access to company systems
- All incidents affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability

---

## 3. Control Objectives

- Ensure incidents are identified, triaged, contained, and resolved quickly
- Minimize business impact and data loss
- Maintain clear communication and evidence for internal and external stakeholders

---

## 4. Control Requirements

### 4.1 Incident Response Plan

- A documented incident response plan (IRP) is maintained and reviewed at least annually.
- The IRP defines severity levels, response timelines, and escalation paths.

### 4.2 Detection and Reporting

- All personnel must report suspected incidents within 24 hours of discovery.
- Centralized channels are maintained for reporting (e.g., ticketing system, dedicated email, hotline).

### 4.3 Triage and Classification

- Incidents are logged and assigned a severity level within 24 hours of report.
- Response actions follow predefined playbooks aligned to incident type and severity.

### 4.4 Containment, Eradication, and Recovery

- Containment actions are initiated based on severity and risk to operations.
- Root cause remediation is documented, validated, and tracked to closure.
- Systems are restored using validated backups or hardened configurations.

### 4.5 Communication and Escalation

- Management is notified of all high severity incidents within 4 hours of confirmation.
- Legal, compliance, and privacy stakeholders are engaged for incidents involving sensitive data.
- Customer or regulator notifications follow contractual and legal obligations.

### 4.6 Evidence Preservation

- Incident evidence (logs, images, tickets) is preserved with chain-of-custody practices.
- Access to evidence is restricted to authorized responders.

### 4.7 Post-Incident Review

- A post-incident review is completed for high severity incidents within 10 business days.
- Lessons learned and corrective actions are documented and tracked.

### 4.8 Third-Party Incidents

- Vendors must notify the company of relevant incidents within contractually defined timelines.
- Third-party incidents affecting company data follow the same triage and response workflow.

### 4.9 Testing and Exercises

- Incident response tabletop exercises are conducted at least annually.
- Playbooks are updated based on test outcomes and real incidents.

### 4.10 Exceptions

- Exceptions require documented risk assessment and approval by Security or Compliance leadership.

---

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Security/Compliance Lead | Owns IRP, coordinates response, approves external notifications |
| Incident Commander | Leads response activities and decision-making |
| IT/Engineering | Execute containment and remediation actions |
| Legal/Privacy | Assess notification obligations and coordinate communications |
| All Personnel | Report incidents promptly |

---

## 6. Evidence & Audit Artifacts

- Incident tickets and timelines
- Incident response plan and playbooks
- Forensic logs and system images
- Post-incident review reports
- Tabletop exercise results

---

## 7. Metrics

- Mean time to detect (MTTD)
- Mean time to contain (MTTC)
- Mean time to recover (MTTR)
- Number of incidents by severity and root cause
- Percentage of incidents with completed post-mortems

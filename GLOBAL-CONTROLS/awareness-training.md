# Security Awareness Training Controls

**Effective Date:** December 1, 2026  
**Control Owner:** Security or Compliance Lead  
**Target Audience:** All employees, contractors, and long-term vendors with system access  
**Classification:** Internal Use

---

## 1. Purpose

Establish a consistent, measurable security awareness program that reduces human risk, reinforces policy compliance, and supports regulatory and customer requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

These controls apply to:

- All personnel with access to company systems, data, or facilities
- All environments where company data is processed or stored
- All business units and subsidiaries

---

## 3. Control Objectives

- Ensure all personnel understand security responsibilities and acceptable use
- Reduce phishing, social engineering, and data handling risks
- Maintain auditable records of training and acknowledgments

---

## 4. Control Requirements

### 4.1 Program Governance

- A formal security awareness program is maintained and reviewed at least annually.
- Program content is aligned to applicable policies, risk assessments, and regulatory needs.

### 4.2 Onboarding Training

- New hires and contractors must complete security awareness training within 7 days of start date.
- Access to production systems is not granted until onboarding training is completed, unless a documented exception is approved.

### 4.3 Annual Refresher Training

- All personnel must complete refresher training at least once every 12 months.
- Training content is updated when material changes occur in policies, risks, or technology.

### 4.4 Role-Based Training

- Additional role-based training is required for high-risk roles (e.g., engineers, administrators, finance, HR, support).
- Role-based training is completed within 30 days of assuming a high-risk role and annually thereafter.

### 4.5 Phishing and Social Engineering Exercises

- Simulated phishing or social engineering exercises are conducted at least quarterly.
- Individuals who fail simulations must complete targeted remedial training within 14 days.

### 4.6 Policy Acknowledgment

- Personnel must acknowledge key policies (Acceptable Use, Data Classification, Access Control, Incident Reporting) during onboarding and after material updates.

### 4.7 Completion Tracking and Enforcement

- Training completion is tracked in a centralized system with timestamps and user identity.
- Overdue training is escalated to management after 14 days past due.

### 4.8 Third-Party Training

- Long-term contractors and vendors with system access must complete training equivalent to internal requirements or provide evidence of comparable training.

### 4.9 Exceptions

- Any exceptions to these controls must be documented, risk-assessed, time-bound, and approved by Security or Compliance leadership.

---

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Security/Compliance Lead | Owns program content, delivery, and metrics |
| People Ops / HR | Ensures onboarding completion and records |
| Managers | Enforce training completion and remediation |
| All Personnel | Complete training and report suspicious activity |

---

## 6. Evidence & Audit Artifacts

- Training completion reports (CSV/PDF exports)
- Phishing simulation reports and remediation logs
- Policy acknowledgment records
- Program review and content update history

---

## 7. Metrics

- Completion rate by department and role
- Phishing simulation failure rate and trend
- Mean time to remediate training failures
- Percentage of overdue training over time

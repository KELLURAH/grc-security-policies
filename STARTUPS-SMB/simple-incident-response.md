# Simple Incident Response Policy

**Effective Date:** October 2025  
**Policy Owner:** Security Lead or Founder  
**Target Audience:** All employees, contractors, and founders  
**Classification:** Internal Use

---

## Executive Summary

This Simple Incident Response Policy provides a clear, step-by-step process for identifying, reporting, and responding to security incidents. It is designed for startups and small teams to minimize damage, recover quickly, and meet basic regulatory and customer requirements.

---

## 1. Policy Purpose & Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

- Ensure all incidents are handled quickly and effectively
- Minimize business impact and data loss
- Meet legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, founders, and contractors
- All company systems, data, and devices
- All types of security incidents (e.g., data breach, malware, lost device, unauthorized access)

---

## 2. What is a Security Incident?

A security incident is any event that may compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company data, systems, or services. Examples include:

- Lost or stolen laptop or phone
- Malware or ransomware infection
- Unauthorized access to accounts or data
- Accidental data exposure or sharing
- Phishing or social engineering attack

---

## 3. Incident Response Steps

### 3.1 Identify

- Be alert for signs of incidents (alerts, suspicious emails, lost devices, etc.)
- If in doubt, treat as a potential incident

### 3.2 Report

- Immediately report any suspected incident to the Security Lead or Founder
- Use the fastest method (call, message, email)
- Provide details: what happened, when, which systems or data are involved

### 3.3 Contain

- Disconnect affected devices from the network (Wi-Fi, VPN)
- Do not turn off the device unless instructed
- Change passwords for affected accounts

### 3.4 Eradicate & Recover

- Remove malware or unauthorized access (with help from IT/Security)
- Restore data from backups if needed
- Reinstall or reset affected systems

### 3.5 Notify

- Security Lead/Founder determines if customers, partners, or regulators must be notified
- Follow legal and contractual notification requirements

### 3.6 Review & Improve

- Document what happened and how it was handled
- Update policies, training, or controls to prevent recurrence

---

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role                | Responsibility                                 |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Security Lead       | Coordinate response, assess impact, notify as needed |
| Founders/Executives | Support response, approve notifications        |
| Employees           | Report incidents, assist as needed             |
| Contractors         | Report incidents, cooperate with response      |

---

## 5. Training & Awareness

- All staff must know how to recognize and report incidents
- Annual refresher training required

---

## 6. Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Know how to report an incident
- [ ] Disconnect affected devices if needed
- [ ] Change passwords after an incident
- [ ] Restore from backup if required
- [ ] Document and review every incident
